What To TrackThe metrics that matter

The handful of numbers that tell you whether the programme is healthy and where the work is.

MetricWhat it tells youHow to pull it
Referral funnel Health of the pipeline this month — how many referrals are at each stage. Count referrals for the current month, grouped by referral state (signup → onboarding-complete → achieved).
New first-time referrers Fresh supply — customers referring for the very first time. Find each referrer's first-ever referral and keep those whose first one is dated this month.
Share rate What share of active customers are referring at all. Of customers who had a successful payment this month, what fraction also referred someone this month.
Gifts owed Dispatch workload — who qualified and is waiting on a gift. List referrals in the achieved state, then cross-check the dispatch/tracking sheet for what's actually shipped.
$30 credits owed Finance signal — the wallet-credit outlay for the month. Count referees who reached onboarding-complete this month, times $30.
Power referrers Who to nurture — the customers driving the most achieved referrals. Rank referrers by their count of achieved referrals, all-time.

How To QueryThe basics, every time

Six habits that keep referral numbers correct. They apply to almost every query above.

  1. Start in the analytics tool on the referrals data — a new question or a copy of an existing one.
  2. Think in IST months. Bucket by month in India time, not UTC, or your month boundaries will be off.
  3. Filter by referral state. The three states run signup-complete → onboarding-complete → achieved. Pick the stage the metric is about.
  4. Drop noise. Exclude internal/test accounts and any referral row that has no referrer attached.
  5. Dedupe referees. Count each referee once, against their earliest referrer, so a re-attributed referral isn't double-counted.
  6. Gifts need the sheet. The database tells you who qualified; whether a parcel actually shipped lives in the tracking sheet (see Sheets & IDs). Always join the two for a "where's my gift" question.
